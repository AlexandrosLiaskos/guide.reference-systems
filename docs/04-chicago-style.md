# Chicago Style (17th Edition)

Chicago offers two systems: **Notes-Bibliography (NB)** and **Author-Date**. This guide covers both.

---

# Notes-Bibliography System

Uses footnotes or endnotes with a bibliography.

## Inline Citation Format

**Superscript number** in text pointing to footnote/endnote.

Text example: According to recent research,ยน the findings were significant.

| Scenario | First Note | Shortened Note |
|----------|------------|----------------|
| One author | 1. John Smith, *Title* (Place: Publisher, Year), Page. | 1. Smith, *Title*, Page. |
| Two authors | 1. John Smith and Mary Jones, *Title*... | 1. Smith and Jones, *Title*, Page. |
| Three authors | 1. Smith, Jones, and Brown, *Title*... | 1. Smith, Jones, and Brown, *Title*, Page. |
| Four+ authors | 1. John Smith et al., *Title*... | 1. Smith et al., *Title*, Page. |

**Order:** Alphabetical by author surname
**Spacing:** Single-spaced entries, blank line between

# Author-Date System

Similar to APA. Uses parenthetical citations.

## Inline Citation Format

**Basic format:** `(Author Year, Page)`

| Scenario | Inline Format | Example |
|----------|---------------|---------|
| One author | (Author Year, Page) | (Smith 2024, 45) |
| Two authors | (Author and Author Year, Page) | (Smith and Jones 2024, 45) |
| Three authors | (Author, Author, and Author Year) | (Smith, Jones, and Brown 2024) |
| Four+ authors | (Author et al. Year) | (Smith et al. 2024) |
| No author | (Title Year) | (*Research Report* 2024) |
| No date | (Author n.d.) | (Smith n.d.) |

---

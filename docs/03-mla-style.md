# MLA Style (9th Edition)

## Inline Citation Format

**Basic format:** `(Author Page)` — no comma, no "p."

| Scenario | Inline Format | Example |
|----------|---------------|---------|
| One author | (Author Page) | (Smith 45) |
| Two authors | (Author and Author Page) | (Smith and Jones 45) |
| Three+ authors | (First Author et al. Page) | (Smith et al. 45) |
| Organization | (Organization Page) | (United Nations 12) |
| No author | ("Shortened Title" Page) | ("Climate Change" 23) |
| No page number | (Author) | (Smith) |
| Multiple sources | (Author Page; Author Page) | (Smith 45; Jones 67) |
| Same author, multiple works | (Author, *Short Title* Page) | (Smith, *Research* 45) |

---

## Bibliography Format & Examples

**Title:** "Works Cited" (centered)
**Order:** Alphabetical by author surname
**Spacing:** Double-spaced, hanging indent

---

### Books

**Format - Single Author:**
```
Author Last, First. Title of Book: Subtitle. Publisher, Year.
```
**Example:**
```
Smith, John David. The Complete Guide to Research: Methods and Practice. Academic Press, 2024.
```

---

**Format - Two Authors:**
```
Author Last, First, and First Last. Title of Book. Publisher, Year.
```
**Example:**
```
Johnson, Mary, and Kevin Williams. Research Methods Explained. Sage Publications, 2023.
```

---

**Format - Three+ Authors:**
```
Author Last, First, et al. Title of Book. Publisher, Year.
```
**Example:**
```
Adams, Alice, et al. Collaborative Research Methods. Oxford University Press, 2024.
```

---

**Format - Edited Book:**
```
Editor Last, First, editor. Title of Book. Publisher, Year.
```
**Example:**
```
Martinez, Robert, editor. Handbook of Research Methods. Routledge, 2024.
```

---

**Format - Chapter in Edited Book:**
```
Author Last, First. "Title of Chapter." Title of Book, edited by First Last, Publisher, Year, pp. #-#.
```
**Example:**
```
Thompson, Sarah. "Data Analysis Techniques." Handbook of Research Methods, edited by Robert Martinez, Routledge, 2024, pp. 123-56.
```

---

**Format - Edition Other Than First:**
```
Author Last, First. Title of Book. Edition, Publisher, Year.
```
**Example:**
```
Smith, John. Research Basics. 4th ed., Academic Press, 2024.
```

---

**Format - Translated Book:**
```
Author Last, First. Title of Book. Translated by First Last, Publisher, Year.
```
**Example:**
```
García, María. The Art of Discovery. Translated by James Wilson, Academic Press, 2024.
```

---

### Journal Articles

**Format - Print Journal:**
```
Author Last, First. "Title of Article." Journal Name, vol. #, no. #, Year, pp. #-#.
```
**Example:**
```
Williams, Karen. "Effects of Methodology on Outcomes." Journal of Research, vol. 45, no. 3, 2024, pp. 234-51.
```

---

**Format - Online Journal with DOI:**
```
Author Last, First. "Title of Article." Journal Name, vol. #, no. #, Year, pp. #-#. DOI.
```
**Example:**
```
Brown, Michael. "Digital Research Methods." Research Quarterly, vol. 12, no. 2, 2023, pp. 45-67. https://doi.org/10.1000/example.
```

---

**Format - Online Journal (Database):**
```
Author Last, First. "Title of Article." Journal Name, vol. #, no. #, Year, pp. #-#. Database Name.
```
**Example:**
```
Chen, Robert. "Innovative Approaches." Science Journal, vol. 8, no. 1, 2024, pp. 12-28. JSTOR.
```

---

### Websites

**Format - Webpage with Author:**
```
Author Last, First. "Title of Page." Website Name, Publisher, Day Month Year, URL.
```
**Example:**
```
Johnson, Thomas. "Understanding Research Methods." Research Hub, Research Foundation, 15 Mar. 2024, www.researchhub.org/methods.
```

---

**Format - Webpage (Organization Author):**
```
Organization Name. "Title of Page." Website Name, Day Month Year, URL.
```
**Example:**
```
World Health Organization. "Research Guidelines." WHO, 10 Jan. 2024, www.who.int/guidelines.
```

---

**Format - Webpage No Author:**
```
"Title of Page." Website Name, Publisher, Day Month Year, URL.
```
**Example:**
```
"Research Strategies for Students." Academic Resource, 5 Feb. 2024, www.example.com/strategies.
```

---

**Format - Webpage No Date:**
```
Author Last, First. "Title of Page." Website Name, URL. Accessed Day Month Year.
```
**Example:**
```
Smith, Rachel. "Data Collection Methods." Research Portal, www.example.com/data. Accessed 20 Mar. 2024.
```

---

### Newspapers & Magazines

**Format - Print Newspaper:**
```
Author Last, First. "Title of Article." Newspaper Name, Day Month Year, pp. #.
```
**Example:**
```
Martinez, Susan. "New Research Findings Released." The New York Times, 20 Mar. 2024, pp. A1+.
```

---

**Format - Online Newspaper:**
```
Author Last, First. "Title of Article." Newspaper Name, Day Month Year, URL.
```
**Example:**
```
Johnson, Lisa. "Study Reveals New Insights." The Washington Post, 20 Mar. 2024, www.washingtonpost.com/example.
```

---

**Format - Magazine (Print):**
```
Author Last, First. "Title of Article." Magazine Name, Month Year, pp. #-#.
```
**Example:**
```
Brown, Kate. "The Science of Discovery." Scientific American, Apr. 2024, pp. 42-49.
```

---

**Format - Magazine (Online):**
```
Author Last, First. "Title of Article." Magazine Name, Day Month Year, URL.
```
**Example:**
```
Davis, Mark. "Innovation in Research." Time, 15 Mar. 2024, www.time.com/innovation.
```

---

### Reports

**Format - Government Report:**
```
Organization. Title of Report. Publisher, Year.
```
**Example:**
```
National Institute of Health. Annual Research Report 2024. U.S. Government Printing Office, 2024.
```

---

**Format - Technical Report:**
```
Author Last, First. Title of Report. Organization, Year.
```
**Example:**
```
Smith, James, and Mary Jones. Analysis of Outcomes. Research Institute, 2024.
```

---

### Dissertations & Theses

**Format - Published (Database):**
```
Author Last, First. Title of Dissertation. Year. University, PhD dissertation. Database Name.
```
**Example:**
```
Williams, Robert. Research Methodology in Practice. 2024. University of Michigan, PhD dissertation. ProQuest.
```

---

**Format - Unpublished:**
```
Author Last, First. Title of Dissertation. Year. University, PhD dissertation.
```
**Example:**
```
Chen, Linda. Innovative Research Approaches. 2024. Stanford University, PhD dissertation.
```

---

### Conference Papers

**Format:**
```
Author Last, First. "Title of Paper." Conference Name, Day Month Year, Location.
```
**Example:**
```
Johnson, Kevin. "New Approaches to Data Analysis." Annual Research Convention, 15-18 May 2024, Chicago.
```

---

### Audiovisual Media

**Format - Film:**
```
Title of Film. Directed by First Last, Production Company, Year.
```
**Example:**
```
Inception. Directed by Christopher Nolan, Warner Bros., 2010.
```

---

**Format - YouTube Video:**
```
Author/Channel. "Title of Video." YouTube, Day Month Year, URL.
```
**Example:**
```
TED. "The Future of Research." YouTube, 10 Mar. 2024, www.youtube.com/watch?v=xxxxx.
```

---

**Format - Podcast Episode:**
```
"Title of Episode." Podcast Name, hosted by First Last, Season #, Episode #, Publisher, Day Month Year.
```
**Example:**
```
"Research Methods Explained." The Research Podcast, hosted by John Smith, season 2, episode 45, Spotify, 5 Mar. 2024.
```

---

**Format - TV Episode:**
```
"Title of Episode." Series Name, created by First Last, season #, episode #, Production Company, Year.
```
**Example:**
```
"The Discovery." Science Explorers, created by Amy Williams, season 2, episode 5, Netflix, 2024.
```

---

**Format - Music/Song:**
```
Artist Last, First. "Song Title." Album Title, Publisher, Year.
```
**Example:**
```
Swift, Taylor. "Anti-Hero." Midnights, Republic Records, 2022.
```

---

### Social Media

**Format - Tweet/X Post:**
```
Author Last, First [or @username]. "Content up to 20 words." Twitter, Day Month Year, URL.
```
**Example:**
```
@BillGates. "Excited to share new research findings on climate change solutions." Twitter, 10 Mar. 2024, twitter.com/BillGates/status/xxxxx.
```

---

**Format - Facebook Post:**
```
Author/Page Name. "Content up to 20 words." Facebook, Day Month Year, URL.
```
**Example:**
```
NASA. "New images from the Mars rover reveal surprising geological features." Facebook, 15 Mar. 2024, www.facebook.com/NASA/posts/xxxxx.
```

---

**Format - Instagram Post:**
```
Author [or @username]. Description of content. Instagram, Day Month Year, URL.
```
**Example:**
```
@natgeo. Photograph of aurora borealis in Iceland. Instagram, 12 Mar. 2024, www.instagram.com/p/xxxxx.
```

---

### Legal Sources

**Format - Court Case:**
```
Name v. Name. Volume Reporter Page. Court, Year.
```
**Example:**
```
Brown v. Board of Education. 347 U.S. 483. Supreme Court of the US, 1954.
```

---

**Format - Law/Statute:**
```
Title of Act. Public Law #. Statute Volume. Year.
```
**Example:**
```
Americans with Disabilities Act. Public Law 101-336. 104 Stat. 327. 1990.
```

---

### Software & Apps

**Format:**
```
Developer. Title of Software. Version, Publisher, Year.
```
**Example:**
```
Microsoft Corporation. Microsoft Excel. Version 16.0, Microsoft, 2024.
```

---

### Data Sets

**Format:**
```
Author Last, First. "Title of Data Set." Publisher, Year, DOI or URL.
```
**Example:**
```
Smith, James. "National Survey Data 2024." ICPSR, 2024, https://doi.org/10.1000/example.
```

---

### Interviews

**Format - Published Interview:**
```
Interviewee Last, First. Interview by First Last. Publication, Day Month Year.
```
**Example:**
```
Obama, Barack. Interview by David Letterman. My Next Guest, 12 Jan. 2024.
```

---

**Format - Personal Interview:**
```
Interviewee Last, First. Personal interview. Day Month Year.
```
**Example:**
```
Smith, John. Personal interview. 15 Mar. 2024.
```

---

### Images & Artwork

**Format:**
```
Artist Last, First. Title of Work. Year. Medium. Museum/Location.
```
**Example:**
```
Monet, Claude. Water Lilies. 1906. Oil on canvas. Art Institute of Chicago.
```
